Proof

Let \(x\) be an odd integer. Then by the definition of odd integer, there exists an integer \(k\) such that \(x=2k+1\text{.}\) Now consider \(x+1\text{.}\) We may substitute \(x=2k+1\text{,}\) which gives \(x+1 = (2k+1)+1\text{.}\) Using algebra, we have

\begin{equation*} x+1 = (2k+1)+1 = 2k+2 = 2(k+1)\text{.} \end{equation*}

Let \(m=k+1\text{.}\) Since \(k\) is an integer, \(m\) is also an integer. Thus, we have that \(x+1=2m\text{,}\) where \(m\) is an integer. This is exactly what it means for \(x+1\) to be even.
