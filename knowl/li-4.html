Item.

\(p\) and \(q\text{:}\) This is true exactly when both are true. Thus, this is false when at least one statement is false. Hence, the negation is true when at least one statement is true. To shuffle the “not” around a bit, that means we need at least one of “not \(p\)” and “not \(q\)” to be true, so the negation is (not \(p\)) and (not \(q\)).
