Given an ordered basis \(\beta =\{ \vec{v}_1, ...,\vec{v}_k \}\) of a vector space \(V\text{,}\) the coordinate vector of \(\vec{x}\) relative to \(\beta\), denoted \([\vec{x}]_\beta\text{,}\) is a vector of the coefficients of the unique way to write \(\vec{x}\) as a linear combination of \(\beta\text{.}\) Namely, if \(\vec{x} = c_1 \vec{v_1}+c_2 \vec{v_2} +...+c_k \vec{v_k}\text{,}\) then \([\vec{x}]_\beta = \colvec{c_1\\ c_2\\ \vdots\\ c_k}\text{.}\)
