Task 1.3.b.

Next, we have to show that our \(x\) is unique. To do so, you assume that \(x_1,x_2\in\mathbb{R}\) both have the property required. Namely, that \((ax_1+1)/(cx_1) = r\) and \((ax_1+1)/(cx_2) = r\text{.}\) Now you use algebra to show that \(x_1=x_2\text{,}\) which means that the \(x\) you came up with originally really is unique.
