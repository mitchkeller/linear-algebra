  1. If \(A\) and \(B\) are \(n\) by \(n\text{,}\) then \(\det(AB)=\det(A) \det(B)\text{.}\)

  2. The determinant of an upper or lower triangular matrix is the product of its diagonal entries.

    \begin{gather*} \det(L)=\prod_{i=1}^n (L)_{i,i}\\ \det(U)=\prod_{i=1}^n (U)_{i,i} \end{gather*}

  3. The determinant of a diagonal matrix is the product of its diagonal entries. If \(D\) is diagonal, then

    \begin{equation*} \det(D)=\prod_{i=1}^n (D)_{i,i}\text{.} \end{equation*}

  4. \(\displaystyle \det(A)=\det(A^T)\)

  5. A matrix \(A\) is invertible iff \(\det(A)\neq 0\text{.}\)
