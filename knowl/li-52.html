The set of all polynomials (in variable \(t\)) denoted \(\mathbb{P}\text{.}\) %\item \((\mathbb{R})^\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) the set of bi-infinite sequences of real numbers. If \(x \in (\mathbb{R})^\mathbb{Z}\text{,}\) then \(x=... x_{-3},x_{-2},x_{-1}, x_0,x_1,x_2,x_3,...\) where \(x_i \in \mathbb{R}\text{.}\) Scalar multiplication and vector addition are done coordinate-wise in this vector space, i.e.
\begin{equation*} x+y = ..., x_{-3}+y_{-3} , x_{-2}+y_{-2} , x_{-1}+y_{-1} , x_0+y_0 , x_1+y_1 , x_2+x_2 , x_3+y_3,... \end{equation*}
