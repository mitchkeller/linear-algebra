\((\Rightarrow)\) Let \(x+4\) be an odd integer. Hence there exists an integer \(n\) such that \(x+4=2n+1\text{.}\) By adding three to both sides of this equation, we have \(x+7 = 2n+4\text{.}\) Factoring the right-hand side gives \(x+7 = 2(n+2)\text{.}\) Hence, since \(x+7\) is twice an integer, we have proved that \(x+7\) is even.

\((\Leftarrow)\) Let \(x+7\) be an even integer. This means that there is an integer such that \(x+7=2m\text{.}\) Subtracting \(3\) from both sides gives \(x+4=2m-3\text{.}\) A bit more algebra shows that \(x+4 = 2m-4+1 = 2(m-2)+1\text{.}\) Thus, we have proved that \(x+4\) is an odd integer.
