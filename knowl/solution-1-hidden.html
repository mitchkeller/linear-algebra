Let \(x\) and \(y\)be odd integers. Hence there exist integers \(k\) and \(n\) such that \(x=2k+1\) and \(y=2n+1\text{.}\) Multiplying, we have

\begin{equation*} xy=(2k+1)(2n+1) = 4kn+2k+2n+1 = 2(2kn+k+n)+1\text{.} \end{equation*}

Since \(k\) and \(n\) are integers, we know that \(2kn+k+n\) is an integer. Thus, we see that \(xy\) is an odd integer.

(Note that it would be completely reasonable to do something like let \(m=2kn+k+n\) if you wanted to make the form of an odd integer clearer, but I think this proof would be fine as written.)
